# Model QGE2.2: Quantitative genetics model for sus./inf./rec. with demographic stratification

## Objective
- Introduce disease transmission experiment for estimating quantitative genetic contributions to susceptibility, infectivity and recoverability.
- A population of half-sib progeny is randomly allocated into contact groups.

## Model
- A single individual-based species 'Animal' is created.
- This contains classifications 'DS', 'Group' and 'sex'.
- 'DS' contains tfour compartments: susceptible $S$, exposed $E$, infected $I$ and recovered $R$.
- 'Group' contains 200 contact groups.
- A transmission rate $β_s$ determines how fast individuals become infected in each sex.
- Individual effects [ga] (for genetic contribution) and [ge] (for environmental contribution) characterise individual variation in susceptibility.
- Individual effects [fa] (for genetic contribution) and [fe] (for environmental contribution) characterise individual variation in infectivity.
- Individual effects [ra] (for genetic contribution) and [re] (for environmental contribution) characterise individual variation in recoverability.
- A fixed effect 〈w〉 multiplies susceptibility.
- $Ω^ga,ga$, $Ω^fa,fa$ and $Ω^ra,ra$ give genetic variances.
- $ω^ga,fa$, $ω^ga,ra$ and $ω^fa,ra$ gives genetic correlations between traits.
- $Ω^ge,ge$, $Ω^fe,fe$ and $Ω^re,re$ give environmental variances.
- $ω^ge,fe$, $ω^ge,re$ and $ω^fe,re$ gives environmental correlation between traits.

## Population
- Each contact group consists of 10 individuals, two of which have been randomly infected.
- The population is made up of 200 sires who each have 10 progeny (from randomly mated dams). These 2000 progeny are randomly allocated across the contact groups.
