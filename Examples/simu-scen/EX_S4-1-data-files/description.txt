# Model S4.1: Test-and-cull

## Objective
- Introduce the test-and-cull protocol used to reduce disease prevalence.
- This is a method in which the disease status of individuals are periodically checked with a disease diagnostic test, and positive individuals are removed from the population.

## Model
- A single population-based species 'People' is created.
- This contains a classification 'DS' which stands for 'disease status'.
- DS contains three compartments: susceptible $S$, infected $I$ and recovered $R$. Together they are known as the “SIR model”.
- A transmission rate $β$ determines the rate at which individuals become infected.
- A recovery rate $γ$ determines the rate at which individuals recover.
- The disease diagnostic test has a sensitivity $Se=0.5$ and specificity $Sp=0.99$.
- Checks are periodically taken every 10 time units but onty 50% of individuals are tested.
- Individuals are removed 1 time unit after a positive test.

## Population
- This consists of 100 initially susceptible individuals and one infected.
